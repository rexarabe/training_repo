Building on the Basics

The chapters in this book have covered the fundamental components of a Linux system, from low-level kernel and process organization, to networking, to some of the tool used to build software.
With all of that behind you, what can you do now?
Quite a lot, as it turns out!
Because Linux supports nearly every kind of non-proprietary programming environment, it's only natural that a plethora of applications is available.
Let's look at a few application ares where Linux excels and see how what you've learned in this book relates.
